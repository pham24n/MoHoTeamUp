Introduction

Below we've put together advices from many seniors and alums to help you get started!

How to come up with an idea for your project?

Remember what your goal is, especially when you are doing this for the first time, trying to learn new skills in a specific language? applying CS knowledge to your own fun idea? Whatever it is, here are a few things to help you navigate:

  • Are you having fun? What is something you are excited about, what is something you care about?
  • Is the project providing you with value?
  • Start simple — You know your own limit. It’s okay to feel a little ambitious and try cool complex projects to make a shiny portfolio, but be careful not to overwhelm yourself with new technologies and frameworks then give up midway.

... and many others you can find at Addtional Resources below!

How to get started once you have an idea?

Plan plan plan:

  • What is the goal of this project? How long are you planning to work on it?
  • What do you want your product to look like, what features it will include?
  • What technology are you going to use?

→ Make a task list , set deadlines , draft a sketch of your product.

Once you feel prepared, code away

  • If you are already familiar with the technology, plan an implementation strategy (see below for more guidance)
  • If you are learning new technology, LinkedIn Learning and tutorial blogs on Medium or Hackernoon often offer straightforward explanation to help you along the way
